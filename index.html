The average wind speed is measured in metres per second (m/sec). All states have a wind speed ranging from 3.00 to 4 m/sec. However, Tasmania is shown to be an outlier with an average wind speed of 7.60 m/sec. This is evident through the location of Tasmania in comparison to the rest of the states; Tamsmania is the furthest away from the equator.

South Australia follows with a wind speed close to 5 m/sec. The geographical location in the Southern Ocean may result in having higher wind speeds. New South Wales has the lowest average wind speed, being just above 3m/sec.

Evapotranspiration is a combination of processes by which water moves from the land surface to the atmosphere.

The more sun in an area, the more heat that hits the surface of the Earth, evaporating water from the land. Evidently, Victoria and Tasmania have the least amount of evapotranspiration with just above 2.00mm. From the data, both those states have the lowest Max and Min temperature, in comparison to the rest of Australia. This is shown in the line graph below.

Northern Territory has the highest temperature, with the max and min temperature being 34.94 and 21.56 degrees celsius respectively. Therefore, the Northern Territory has the highest evapotranspiration of 6.24. We can conclude that the amount of water that is evaporated from a region, is dependent on the temperature. The colder the state is, the less evaporation there is.
